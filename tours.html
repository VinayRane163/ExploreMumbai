Tourist Spots in Mumbai

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an iconic monument built during the British Raj. It is a popular starting point for tourists visiting Mumbai.

Rs 999 only

Marine Drive

Marine Drive is a picturesque promenade along the Arabian Sea. It offers stunning views of the sea and the city skyline.

Rs 999 only

Elephanta Caves

Located on Elephanta Island, these ancient caves house rock-cut sculptures and temples dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Rs 999 only

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSMT)

Formerly known as Victoria Terminus, this historic railway station is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its stunning architecture.

Rs 999 only

Haji Ali Dargah

Haji Ali Dargah is a mosque and tomb located on an islet in the Arabian Sea. It is a beautiful example of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Rs 999 only

Juhu Beach

Juhu Beach is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike, known for its sandy shores and vibrant atmosphere.

Rs 999 only

Siddhivinayak Temple

Siddhivinayak Temple is a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha. It attracts a large number of devotees and tourists.

Rs 999 only

Sanjay Gandhi National Park

This park offers a refreshing escape from the urban hustle, with diverse flora and fauna. The Kanheri Caves are also located within the park.

Rs 999 only

Colaba Causeway

Colaba Causeway is a bustling street market known for its shops, boutiques, and vibrant street food. It's a great place for shopping enthusiasts.

Rs 999 only

Worli Sea Face

Worli Sea Face is a popular promenade offering panoramic views of the Arabian Sea. It's a serene place to relax and enjoy the sea breeze.

Rs 999 only

Chowpatty Beach

Chowpatty Beach is famous for its lively atmosphere, street food stalls, and cultural events. It's a favorite spot for locals and tourists alike.

Rs 999 only
